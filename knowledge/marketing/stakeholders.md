# Stakeholders & Approval Matrix — Marketing Project

> **Template file.** Copy to `~/.project-hub/knowledge/marketing/stakeholders.md`
> and replace placeholders with your project's actual team.

## Purpose

Defines who is involved in a marketing campaign or project, what each role owns,
and who must approve which assets before production or go-live.

---

## Internal Roles

### Brand Manager

**Owns:** Brand integrity, tone of voice, visual identity compliance

Key responsibilities:
- Reviews all creative assets against brand guidelines
- Approves final concepts before production
- Escalates brand misuse or off-strategy creative

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Name | |
| Email | |
| Review SLA | 2 business days |

### Campaign Manager

**Owns:** End-to-end campaign execution, timeline, budget tracking

Key responsibilities:
- Manages campaign plan and asset production schedule
- Coordinates briefings to agency, designers, media buyer
- Reports weekly performance to stakeholders
- Owns the brief and is the single point of contact for the campaign

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Name | |
| Email | |

### Legal / Compliance

**Owns:** Regulatory requirements, disclaimers, data privacy, claims substantiation

Key responsibilities:
- Reviews copy for legally compliant claims
- Approves use of testimonials, statistics, and comparisons
- Ensures GDPR / data handling requirements are met in campaign mechanics
- Reviews influencer or partner contracts

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Name | |
| Email | |
| Review SLA | 3 business days |
| Trigger for involvement | Any claim, testimonial, competition, data capture, or regulated product |

### Media Buyer / Planner

**Owns:** Channel selection, media plan, platform management, budget pacing

Key responsibilities:
- Builds and optimises media plan
- Manages platform accounts (Google, Meta, etc.)
- Reports on paid media performance weekly
- Reallocates budget based on performance data

| Email | |

### Designer / Creative Lead

**Owns:** Asset production quality, visual execution

Key responsibilities:
- Produces all creative assets per brief
- Adapts master assets to required formats and sizes
- Ensures assets meet platform specs before handover

## Approval Matrix

Who must review and approve each asset type, and by when.

| Asset Type | Draft Review | Legal Review Required | Final Approval | Deadline Before Live |
|------------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Campaign brief | Campaign Manager | No | Brand Manager | D-21 |
| Creative concepts | Brand Manager | No | Brand Manager + Campaign Manager | D-14 |
| Ad copy (paid) | Campaign Manager | Yes (if claim) | Brand Manager + Legal | D-7 |
| Email copy | Campaign Manager | Yes (if claim) | Brand Manager | D-5 |
| Landing page | Campaign Manager | Yes | Brand Manager + Legal | D-5 |
| Influencer content | Campaign Manager | Yes | Brand Manager | D-3 |
| Press release | Campaign Manager | Yes | Brand Manager + Legal | D-5 |
| Social post (organic) | Campaign Manager | No (unless claim) | Brand Manager | D-2 |
| Video / TV | Brand Manager + Agency | Yes | Brand Manager + Legal | D-10 |

**D = Campaign launch date. D-X = X business days before launch.**

## Escalation

| Situation | Escalate to | Method |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Asset rejected by Brand — tight timeline | Brand Manager + Head of Marketing | Sync call |
| Legal blocks claim — campaign at risk | Legal + Campaign Manager + Head of Marketing | Sync call |
| Agency misses brief deadline | Campaign Manager → Agency Lead → Head of Marketing | Email + call |
| Budget overspend > 10% | Campaign Manager → Head of Marketing | Written report |
