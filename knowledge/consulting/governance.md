## Purpose

Defines how decisions are made, how changes are controlled, and how issues are escalated
in a consulting engagement. Agree and sign off at project kick-off.

---

## Decision Log

Record all material decisions here. "Material" = anything that affects scope, timeline,
budget, deliverable content, or working approach.

| # | Date | Decision | Context / Rationale | Owner | Status |
|---|------|----------|---------------------|-------|--------|
| D-001 | YYYY-MM-DD | [What was decided] | [Why this was decided] | | Open / Closed |
| D-002 | YYYY-MM-DD | | | | |

**Rules:**
- Any decision made in a meeting is logged within 24 hours by the Project Coordinator.
- Disputed decisions are escalated to the Steering Committee.
- Closed decisions can be re-opened only by mutual agreement of both parties.

## Change Request Process

### When a CR is required

A Change Request is required for any change to:
- Scope (additional deliverables, expanded coverage, new workstreams)
- Timeline (extension of any milestone by more than [5] business days)
- Budget (any cost increase)
- Key personnel on either side

Minor clarifications within agreed scope do not require a CR.

### Process Steps

1. **Identify:** Either party identifies a potential scope change.
2. **Log:** Requesting party notifies both engagement leads in writing (email / meeting note).
3. **Assess:** Consultant assesses impact within [3] business days:
   - Change to scope of work
   - Additional effort / cost
   - Timeline impact
4. **Submit CR:** Consultant prepares CR Form and shares with client.
5. **Review:** Client reviews and responds within [3] business days.
6. **Approve / Reject:** Both engagement leads sign. No work starts before sign-off.
7. **Update plan:** Project Coordinator updates plan, budget, and this document.

## Escalation Matrix

Use this matrix when an issue cannot be resolved at the current level within the defined
resolution window.

| Level | Trigger | Owner | Resolution Target | Escalate to |
|-------|---------|-------|------------------|-------------|
| Level 1 — Working | Day-to-day issue, input delay, minor disagreement | Project Coordinator | 2 business days | Level 2 |
| Level 2 — Management | Unresolved after Level 1, scope dispute, resource conflict | Lead Consultant + Project Owner | 5 business days | Level 3 |
| Level 3 — Executive | Unresolved after Level 2, contractual dispute, major scope change | Exec Sponsor + Consulting Principal | Case-by-case | External mediation |

## Steering Committee Charter

### Purpose

The Steering Committee (SteerCo) provides oversight, resolves escalated issues,
and approves material changes. It does not manage day-to-day delivery.

### Composition

| Role | Person | Representing |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Chair | [Executive Sponsor] | Client |
| Member | [Project Owner] | Client |
| Member | [Lead Consultant] | Consultant |
| Optional | [SME / additional client stakeholder] | — |

### Frequency & Format

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Frequency | Monthly / At key milestones |
| Duration | 60 minutes |
| Format | In-person / Video call |
| Quorum | Minimum 3 members including Chair |
| Minutes | Circulated within 48 hours of meeting |

### Standard Agenda

1. Approval of previous minutes (5 min)
2. Progress against milestones — traffic light status (10 min)
3. Budget status (5 min)
4. Risks and issues update (10 min)
5. Change Requests for approval (10 min)
6. Decisions required (10 min)
7. AOB (5 min)
8. Date of next meeting (5 min)

### Decision-Making

- Decisions by consensus where possible.
- If no consensus: Chair has casting vote.
- Decisions recorded in Decision Log within 24 hours.
- Written decisions (via email) acceptable between meetings for time-sensitive items —
  require agreement from all SteerCo members.
