## Process Overview

The merchant onboarding process consists of 6 phases from initial discussion to hypercare.

## Phase 1 — Merchant Discussion

**Goal:** Understand merchant requirements and fit assessment

Key activities:
- Initial merchant discovery call
- Product scope definition (e.g. BNPL, Pay-in-3, Pay-in-X)
- Market and country scope confirmation
- Preliminary volume and integration complexity assessment
- Stakeholder mapping (internal and external)

**Output:** Deal Sheet draft, stakeholder list, preliminary timeline

**Exit criteria:** Commercial team confirms deal viability

---

## Phase 2 — Deviation Assessment

**Goal:** Identify all deviations from standard onboarding parameters

Key activities:
- Deal Sheet review against standard parameter matrix
- Risk assessment (volume, fraud profile, market specifics)
- Legal / compliance check (regulatory requirements per market)
- Technical complexity assessment (custom integration, non-standard flows)
- Formal deviation documentation

**Output:** Deviation register, risk flags, approval requirements

**Exit criteria:** All deviations documented and submitted for approval

---

## Phase 3 — Formal Approval

**Goal:** Secure cross-functional approval to proceed

Key activities:
- Deviation approval by function leads (see Governance)
- Go/No-Go decision by senior stakeholders
- Final scope lock-in
- Onboarding plan sign-off

**Output:** Signed-off onboarding plan, approved deviations

**Exit criteria:** All required approvals obtained, timeline confirmed

---

## Phase 4 — Offer / Contracting Setup

**Goal:** Contract finalization and merchant agreement

Key activities:
- Contract template selection and preparation
- Merchant review and negotiation
- Legal sign-off
- Contract execution

**Output:** Signed merchant contract

**Exit criteria:** Executed contract, implementation can begin

---

## Phase 5 — Contracting & Implementation

**Goal:** Technical integration, configuration, and UAT

Key activities:
- API credentials and sandbox environment provisioning
- Technical integration support
- Risk and fraud rule configuration
- Settlement and payment flow setup
- User Acceptance Testing (UAT)
- UAT sign-off by merchant

**Output:** Completed integration, UAT report, Go-Live checklist

**Exit criteria:** UAT passed, all configuration validated

---

## Phase 6 — Go-Live & Hypercare

**Goal:** Controlled launch and post-launch support

Key activities:
- Go-Live readiness check (all teams)
- Production cutover
- First-transaction monitoring
- Hypercare period (typically 4–6 weeks)
- Escalation path active during hypercare
- Handover to Account Management

**Output:** Live merchant, hypercare report, AM handover document

**Exit criteria:** Stable operations, no critical open items

---

## Standard Timeline

| Phase | Typical Duration |
|-------|-----------------|
| Merchant Discussion | 1–2 weeks |
| Deviation Assessment | 1–2 weeks |
| Formal Approval | 1–2 weeks |
| Offer / Contracting | 2–4 weeks |
| Implementation & UAT | 4–8 weeks |
| Go-Live & Hypercare | 4–6 weeks post-launch |

**Total typical range:** 3–5 months depending on complexity
